For the first big inventor we are going back a bit further than you think, Charles Babbage in 1823 was given £1700 or about £94,336 in todays money by the government to build an autonomous engine to make calculaing income and costs quicker for the rapidly growing empire. he didn't succeed as he miss vitail peice of technology that would be invented over the the next century.

Alan Turing and Tommy flowers were the next peole too take on the challenge oof built a successful computer, during world war II, the smartest mind of the british empire gather in a small place called bletchley park, Milton Keynes. This is where Alan turing worked with amny other people listenig to Nazi communication listening for vitail information, the Nazi being smart used a hard to crack code, german high command used the impossible to crack Lorenz cipher, Aln Turing whist working had an idea to create a machine that autonomously could solve the codes the Germans used, he worked with Tommy Flowers a Post office Engineer to build Colossus which broke the Lorenz cipher and saved countless lives at Normany landing in 1944. Tommy Flowers llive until 1998 and died peacfully, as for Turing he was prosecuted for being gay and in 1954 committed suicide.

Ted Hoff, was an employee at Intel, an came up with the Idea of using a universal processor rather than diffrent design circuits. Hoff along with Masatoshi Shima, Stanley Mazor and Federico Faggin designed and built the Intel 4004, this started the what is now known as the microprocessor revolution. Teff Hoff would become First Intel Fellow, the top technician of the company. Masatoshi Shima would go on to help design the 8008 and 8080 processors. Stanley Mazor worked for intel for a few more years before going on to teach at Stanford and santa clare, then Training director at BEA Systems. Federico Faggin would go on to help design the 8008, 4040 and 8080 microprocessors and the with Masatoshi Shima create the Z80 microprocessor and the Z8 micro controller at Zilog, which he was founder.
