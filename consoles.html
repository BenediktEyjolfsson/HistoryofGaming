Early Consoles

Before Home Consoles became the stanard form of gaming, Arcade machines were the most popular form of gaming. The first machine built was for a game called galaxy game in 1971, but it wasn't until 1872 when Nolan Bushnell and Ted Dabney founded Atari and created Pong. During the 70's and early 80's Arcade game became very popular the world over. The business, by the time home consoles started to appear, was wotrth $8 billion or $21.4 billion ajusted for inflation, this was not to last as home gaming kept advancing the arcade business began to fade and by 1991, revune was down in the US alone to $2.1 Billion or $3.8 billion today

During the 90's Arcade fighter took off becuase of their simply controls allowed for co-op fighters like Mortal combat and street fighter, to be quick to learn and fun to play some what reviving the business for a short while.

Home Consoles

In 1972 Magnavox released the Magnavox Odyssey, this was the first console that was able to connect to the TV, The first design of the Odyssey was to have a huge amount of switches to change the version of the diffrent games that the Odyssey had due to the lack of a CPU, this was scrapped and the final design had the player would switch game version by placing peice of see through plastic over the screen. The console had a moderate release selling roughly 100,000 copies, it became more popular with the release of pong on it's system allowing the game previous available on a single machine for loads of people, was now available for everyone.

The second generation of Consoles saw much improvement from the previous generation, the addition of a microprocessor allowed for many improcement like AI and ROM cartridges so that many games could be played on one console. the major steps were Simple sprites, basic colours either 1-bit or 4-bit and three channel audio. The only major improvement since the second generation is changing from cartridge to disc in the forth generation with the Commodore CDTVbeing the first to fully exclusively use the new media format.

Portable Consloes

The first handheld console was the Microvision in 1979, it had no colour and it had no processor so the all the game funtion were done by the cartridges. there would be a few more handheld consoles to coem but they only had 4 or 5 games on them. Handheld console would not become popular until 1989 when the gameboy was realeased in total 118.69 million sale were made including the 1998 version of the console, the Gameboy color, this console would dominate the portable console market

Nintendo's Gameboy continued to dominate the portable console market with their new console the Gameboy Advanced and DS but only 10 day after the DS's Japan release Sony would bring out the Playstation Portable, although it only sold 80 million compared to the DS's 154 million this was a first for portable consoles as no one had challenged Nintedo's monopoly before.
