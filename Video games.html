Early Video Games

Early Video games were very basic, Pong was one of the first and most simple. Pong consists of two paddles, represented by two bar on either side of the screen, a ball, represented by a square dot, a dotted line to represent the net and the score of each team kept at the top. I would say that is the game that popularised video games. Atari, the creators of pong monoplised on the success of the Pong by creating many diffrent version from 1972 all the way to 1977

From 1978 through the 80s' other big game titles came out such as Space Invaders, asteroids, Pac-man, Frogger and Donkey Kong. These games set the Formula for that era of games 2D with very basic movements and all about the mechanics and not such much the story. The formula was rocked in 1985 when Nintendo made Super Mario Bros., This game changed alot from previous games with a variety of enemies more established characters Mario, the main character, with his younger brother Luigi, if you played multiplayer, Bowser, a evil tortoise like creature, and Princess Toadstool, later renamed Princess Peach. but most importantly it had a story something that was not the forefront of a video game devolpment at the time. this meant they could remake the same game with diffrent levels and a new story, and make a seires. Mario would be the only seires until the 90s' when seires like Sonic the Hedgehog, Doom and street fighter.

Major Steps

One of the first major steps in video game history is colour. Wimbledon, a game released in 1974 by Nutting Associates, It is similar to pong but has two players per team instead of one. Although some game after Wimbledon did used colour, but It wasn't until about 1980 when Pac-man was released that it became an industry standard

Isometric graphics, also known as 2.5D, three-quarter perspective and pseudo-3D, is view that makes 2D model to look 3D, hence the nickname 2.5D. The first game to use this Perspective was the si-fi shooter Zaxxon, which also was the first game to implement shadows. This became a great tool for strategy games, like Civilization when they released Civilization II, this is also true for RPGs(Role Playing Games) like fallout and Diablo, as they could make proper rooms and dungeons to explore.

Online games meant that instead of two or more friends being in the same playing on the same computer or console was now available over the internet, the first game to do this was MIDI Maze released on the Atari ST, it used a MIDI(Musical Instrument Digital Interface) which purpose was to allow a variety of electrical musical instruments, computers and other related devices to connect and communicate with one another. this allowed Atari and Xanth Software F/X to allow 16 players to enjoy the same game at the same time. This also allowed for devolpers to make games where every player could be in the same game all at once knows as MMOs(Massive Multiplayer Online)
